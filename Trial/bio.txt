Biology is the scientific study of living organisms and life processes.

Cells are the basic structural and functional units of life. All living organisms are made of one or more cells.

Photosynthesis is the process by which green plants use sunlight, carbon dioxide, and water to produce glucose and oxygen.

Cellular respiration is the process where cells break down glucose to release energy in the form of ATP.

DNA carries genetic information used for growth, reproduction, and cell function.

Proteins are made of amino acids and perform many functions including enzyme activity and structural support.

Enzymes are biological catalysts that speed up chemical reactions without being consumed.

The nucleus is an organelle that contains DNA and controls cell activities.

Mitochondria are known as the powerhouse of the cell because they generate ATP energy.

The ribosome is responsible for protein synthesis.

Osmosis is the movement of water across a selectively permeable membrane from high water concentration to low water concentration.

Diffusion is the movement of particles from an area of high concentration to low concentration.

Natural selection is the process where organisms better adapted to their environment survive and reproduce more successfully.

Evolution explains how species change over time through genetic variation and selection.

The human heart has four chambers: two atria and two ventricles.

Red blood cells carry oxygen using hemoglobin.

White blood cells help defend the body against infection.

The nervous system transmits signals between different parts of the body using neurons.

Hormones are chemical messengers produced by endocrine glands.

Insulin is a hormone that helps regulate blood sugar levels.

Type 1 diabetes occurs when the pancreas produces little or no insulin.

Viruses are microscopic infectious agents that require a host cell to reproduce.

Bacteria are single-celled microorganisms. Some bacteria are beneficial while others cause disease.

Antibiotics are medicines used to treat bacterial infections but do not work against viruses.

The human brain contains billions of neurons and controls thought, memory, and movement.

Homeostasis is the maintenance of stable internal conditions in the body.
